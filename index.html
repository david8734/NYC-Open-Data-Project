Project Overview

This website is here to help people learn more about health in New York City.

Use the Filter page when you want to look up a specific cause of death.

Use the Analysis page when you want to compare the data with simple charts.

It is meant to be easy to use, so anyone can explore the numbers and see what they mean.

The project shows how data can highlight trends and help people think about public health in the city.

Leading Causes of Death in NYC

This site helps you explore real health data from New York City and learn which causes of death are most common.

You can look at individual records or get a quick overview with charts and summaries.

Why this data matters

These numbers tell a story about health in the city. They make it easier to see which causes appear most often and where they are happening.

How to use this site

Pick a cause on the Filter page to see records that match your choice.

Open the Analysis page when you want to compare results with easy-to-read visuals.

The navigation links at the top help you move between pages.

What each page does

Home: get an overview of the project and how to use it.

Filter: search the data and read details for each record.

Analysis: compare the data with simple charts and summaries.

Data source

The data comes from NYC open sources that track causes of death. The site keeps the information clear and easy to read.
